JavaScript Identifiers / Names :-  

    1) Identifiers are used to name variables and keywords, and functions.
    2) A JavaScript name must begin with :-  
        - A letter (A-Z or a-z)
        - A dollar sign ($)
        - Or an underscore (_)
        Subsequent characters may be letters, digits, underscores, or dollar signs.
    3) Numbers are not allowed as the first character in names.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Note :-   

    1) All JavaScript Identifiers are Case-Sensitive.
        - The variables lastName and lastname, are two different variables:

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ways to Joining Multiple Words :-  

    1) Hyphens (Not Allowed) :-  first-name, last-name, master-card, inter-city, etc.
            - Hyphens are Not Allowed in Javascript. They are Reserved for Subtraction.
    2) Underscores (Not Recommended) :-  first_name, last_name, master_name, inter_city.
    3) PascalCase (For FileName) :-   FirstName, LastName, MasterName, inter_city.
    4) camelCase (For methods, variable) :- firstName, lastName, masterCard, interCity.
